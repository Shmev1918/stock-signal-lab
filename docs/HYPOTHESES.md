# Hypotheses

This is a living backlog of testable investment ideas.

- Companies with declining debt and improving free cash flow outperform over 180 days.
- Companies with improving margins but falling price may be value recovery candidates.
- Momentum is more predictive when revenue growth is positive.
- High valuation is less dangerous when free cash flow growth is accelerating.
- Conservative-quality strategy should outperform in declining markets.
- Growth-momentum strategy should outperform in strong bull markets.
- Value-recovery strategy should find better 180-day rebounds than balanced.
- Insider buying plus improving margins may be stronger than either signal alone.
- Weak 12-month momentum combined with improving fundamentals may indicate opportunity.
- Stocks rated ACCUMULATE should outperform WATCH and AVOID over 180 days.

## Congressional Trading

- Stocks with bipartisan congressional buying outperform SPY over 180 days.
- Stocks with high congressional net buying outperform after disclosure.
- Congressional sells are less predictive than congressional buys.
- Trades from historically predictive politicians outperform generic congressional trades.
- Party-divergent congressional trades have different outcomes than bipartisan trades.
- Delayed disclosures reduce predictive usefulness.
- Congressional buying plus improving fundamentals is stronger than congressional buying alone.
- Congressional buying from members on relevant committees may be more predictive than generic buying.

## Stock Options

- Option flow near earnings may improve short-horizon directionality.
- Elevated implied volatility before earnings may forecast larger realized moves.
- High call/put skew with improving fundamentals may be a bullish continuation signal.
- Covered-call style overlays may improve risk-adjusted returns on low-volatility names.
- Small options universes will produce cleaner results than broad market chains at first.
- IV rank may be more useful than raw implied volatility for comparing contracts across names.
- Liquidity filters may matter more than signal strength for options research quality.
